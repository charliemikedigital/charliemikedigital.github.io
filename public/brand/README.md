# Charlie Mike Digital — Brand Kit

Transparent logo files for use in emails, business cards, letterhead, social profiles, and anywhere else you need to drop the brand on top of someone else's background.

All files have **transparent backgrounds**. Pick the variant that matches the surface you're putting it on.

## Files

| File | Use for |
|---|---|
| `cmd-logo-light-bg.svg` / `.png` | Full lockup (mark + wordmark) on **light backgrounds** — white email signatures, light-themed letterhead, light business cards. |
| `cmd-logo-dark-bg.svg` / `.png` | Full lockup on **dark backgrounds** — dark email themes, dark business cards, dark social headers, photo overlays. |
| `cmd-mark-color.svg` / `.png` | Just the icon (CM monogram in the rounded square), full color. Use as a favicon, social avatar, app icon, or compact watermark. |
| `cmd-mark-cream.svg` / `.png` | Just the icon, no dark square — pure cream chevron + "CM" + red dot. Best for placing the icon on dark photographic backgrounds where the dark square would compete. |

## Format guide

- **SVG** — scales to any size without quality loss. Use this for **business cards, print, signage, anything sized larger than 256px**, or anywhere the receiving tool accepts SVG (most modern design tools do).
- **PNG** — fixed-resolution raster. Lockups are 1440×330, mark icons are 1024×1024. Use this for **email signatures** (Gmail, Outlook etc. don't support SVG inline), **older platforms**, or any tool that rejects SVG.

## Email signature quick guide

In Gmail / Outlook / Apple Mail signature settings:

1. Use `cmd-logo-light-bg.png` (since most emails are read on white backgrounds)
2. Resize to ~200–280 px wide in your signature editor
3. Hyperlink the image to `https://charliemikedigital.com` (or `https://charliemikedigital.github.io`)

## Business card guide

Send `cmd-logo-light-bg.svg` (for light card stock) or `cmd-logo-dark-bg.svg` (for dark / black card stock) to your printer. SVG ensures the print is crisp at any size. The wordmark uses **Instrument Serif** (italic) and **Inter** (semibold) — both Google Fonts, free for commercial use, available at fonts.google.com if your printer asks.

## Color reference

For anyone re-creating brand assets:

- Warm gradient (Mike text + ring): `#ffd79a → #f5b84a → #ff6a5f`
- Ink (dark square): `#1c1a20 → #0d0c10` (vertical gradient)
- Paper (cream lettering): `#efeae0`
- Signal red (target dot, primary CTA): `#ff3b2e`
